TRP Parts

100,000 TRP Parts

UX/UI, Agile, E-commerce Design

TRP is the aftermarket parts division of PACCAR Inc. PACCAR, a Fortune 500 company with more than $19 billion in annual revenue, is known worldwide as the builders of Kenworth, Peterbilt and DAF medium and heavy-duty trucks. With the recent integration of a new product information management system, growing the website’s online parts catalog from 36,000 to more than 100,000 parts and being localized in multiple languages, including English, French and Spanish the company needed to update the website and it's experience.

TRPparts.com has also been internationally recognized for its design and function, with accolades that include a Communicator Award of Excellence, Hermes Creative Awards for website design and mobile experience design, a WebAward for Standard of Excellence, and a MarCom Gold Award.

 

Brief

Paccar Parts, the Fortune 500 parent agency of TRP Parts and an ongoing client, wanted a web redesign and restructure of their online parts catalog.

Overview of the project: Although TRP Parts had an existing site, the parts had more than tippled since the launch of the initial site, and the client additionally needed the introduction of a login account, a loyalty program, and 360 view images into the site.

KPIs:

  • Increase Loyalty Membership

  • Gather meaningful data to know who the Users Are

 

Budget & Research

Budget: 400,000.00

Research:

In addition to the above budget, 20K was approved for Usability Testing during the agile design and development process

In the 4 week long process using design mockups, the research found initial ideations to be friendly, and speak to the audience. There was some feedback in the filtering of the parts and on the parts detail page, which were applied in build.

Problem Statement:

  • How might we improve the TRPparts.com website so that non-technically savvy visitors have a simple and usable experience while browsing for parts online?

  • How might we update the TRPparts.com website so that that the membership is sucessful online and increases the amount of members?

  • How might we refactor the content and code to make sure it's up to standards of WCAG 2.0 AA accessibility?

 

Website before redesign

Challenges:

  • Working within the client's brand and style pallet was difficult due to the colors and audience which during the research phase showed a lack of technical understanding.

  • The target user group was Men, 40-65 with very little experience online

  • Because this was an agile project on strict deadlines, it required me as the UX designer to assist in front-end and styling throughout the process.

Overview of the New Features:

  • Searchable resources, Alternate vs. Related Parts, 360 degree views, part suggestions.

  • Detailed part search including keyword, VMRS, cross-reference, make/model brand search, and filterable attributes and facets on results.

  • The languages were localized completey to regions: USA, Canda, Mexico, and supported English, French, and Spanish.

The Results

The KPI's given to us by client were met, on budget and on time. The beta version 1.0 launched 7.5 months from the start of the project.

The KPI's were met now we can:

  • Increase Loyalty Membership (by creation of Loyalty Portal) and offer personalized offers and retain them in the "Loyalty Loop"

  • Gather meaningful data to know who the users are and leverage targeting messaging and personalized experiences tp our users

  • In addition, the site views increased by 300% and the Target Audience was pleased to have a simple, easy, and friendly experience in looking for their parts.

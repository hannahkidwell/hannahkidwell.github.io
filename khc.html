Central Pacific Bank

#KeepHawaiiCooking

UX/UI, Campaign, Development

This case study is from the early stages of the 2020 Covid Pandemic, in which Hawaii was undergoing high rates of Covid-19 infection. Local restaurants were struggling to remain open. An unlikely candidate to help with this appeared, Central Pacific Bank's Foundation wanted to donate the funds they had accumulated to help both the state of Hawaii and their client's businesses who were struggling.

Within hours of launching this digital campaign, the results were overwhelming. It was a 6-day campaign where the Foundation would pay half of the Consumer's Bill. By the end, over 10k meals were served and $300k was reimbursed. It had over 400k views to the website and it was the #1 trending topic in Hawaii on March 28th. It even garnished mainland coverage.

 

Brief

The client, Central Pacific Bank Foundation that had set aside money to use. They came to us to creatively and thoughtfully offer real-world help and solutions to our customers and all of Hawai‘i’s residents.

 

Budget & Research

Budget: $50,000.00 Funded by Central Pacific Bank

Challenges:

As the clock was ticking away and Covid-19 was wreaking havoc on the economy and the local restaurants, there was a scramble to get the project up and running.

Strategy:

  • Ideation of Campaign

  • Logo / Copy Writing

  • Customer Experience - Across Various Digital Platforms

  • Emails and Brand Site

  • Digital Ads

  • User Experience - Program Website

  • Social Media, Paid, filter, and template for organic

 

Customer Experience

Creative:

“#KeepHawaiiCooking”

Let's Split the Bill and support local restaurants together.

Central Pacific Bank Foundation will reimburse you 50% of the bill for your takeout, drive-through or delivery of food – up to $100 per order – as a mahalo to you for helping us support Hawai‘i’s restaurant community as it navigates challenging times.

UX and Design

User Experience

 

Overview of the Effort:

As a digital-led effort, the User Experience of the website fell on me to design and implement, working with developers to push to production within a weeks time.

During this time, I was also overseeing the CX of the entire digital aspect working with the internal and external teams to coordinate the rules of the promotion and oversee the implementation of these on the various digital platforms.

 
Social Media

The bulk of the daily work fell on the social media team, who was constantly online for weeks speaking to the customers and passing reimbursements and questions about fulfillment made online to the CPB team.

User Experience

 
The Results

Within hours of launching this digital campaign, the results were overwhelming. It was a 6-day campaign where the Foundation would pay half of the Consumer's Bill. By the end, over 10k meals were served and $300k were reimbursed. It had over 400k views to the website and it was the #1 trending topic in Hawaii on March 28th. It even garnished mainland coverage.

The KPI's:

  • 10,162 meals served

  • Amount reimbursed: $301,245.99 (Original commitment: $100,000)

  • 114,200 followers on Instagram

  • 13,488 followers on Facebook

  • 204,765 unique visitors and 427,877 Webpage views

  • Mobile users 81%

  • Only 1 paid print advertisement, the rest of the coverage was free PR
